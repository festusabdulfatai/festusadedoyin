Project 1: AI Agents

AI Agents for Strategic Digital Transformation: Governance, Policy, and Organizational Applications

Editors
Festus Adedoyin, Bournemouth University, United Kingdom

Important Dates
  • Proposals Submission Deadline: February 28, 2026
  • Notification of Acceptance: March 14, 2026
  • Full Chapters Due: May 23, 2026
  • Review Results Returned: July 4, 2026
  • Final Acceptance Notification: August 1, 2026
  • Final Chapter Submission: August 8, 2026
Introduction & Objective

AI agents are reshaping organisational decision-making and operational workflows. This volume examines governance, policy, ethics, and organisational readiness required for responsible deployment of autonomous and semi-autonomous agent systems in public and private sectors. We welcome empirical studies, frameworks, and case studies that bridge technical development and institutional practice.

Recommended Topics

Governance frameworks, multi-agent systems in regulated sectors, accountability mechanisms, human–AI collaboration, data governance and interoperability, case studies in finance, healthcare, energy and public administration.

Submission Procedure

Submit a 1,000–2,000 word proposal by February 28, 2026 via the IGI submission process. Accepted proposals invited to submit full chapters (min. 10,000 words). Full details: IGI author guidelines.

Publisher: IGI Global (anticipated 2027). Inquiries: festusabdulfatai@gmail.com

Project 2: MLE and Human-Centred Innovations

Monitoring, Learning, and Evaluation in the Public Sector: Human-Centered Innovations

Editors
Festus Adedoyin, Bournemouth University, United Kingdom

Important Dates
  • Proposals Submission Deadline: March 7, 2026
  • Notification of Acceptance: March 21, 2026
  • Full Chapters Due: June 20, 2026

Introduction & Objective

This volume explores human-centred approaches and digital innovations to improve Monitoring, Learning and Evaluation (MLE) systems in government and development contexts. We invite applied case studies, methodological innovations, and conceptual contributions that foreground participatory, ethical and adaptive evaluation practices.
